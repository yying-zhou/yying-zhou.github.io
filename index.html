ABOUT

01

2020

Master's Thesis

A Tale of Living

Habitat / Interior Design

This master thesis explores housing models for digital nomads who frequently move between cities. Rather than focusing on architectural form, it investigates psychological needs—comfort, belonging, and loneliness—and translates them into a minimum viable domestic prototype.

RESEARCH QUESTION

How can mobile living/working environments support comfort and reduce loneliness for digital nomads?

KEY TAKEAWAY

Design can be treated as a testable system: needs → minimal unit → combinatorial rules → evidence.

Digital nomads Domesticity Prototype system
02

2019

Competition

Cherry on the Cake

Urban Interior Design / Urban Regeneration

Set on Scalo Farini in Milan—an abandoned rail yard dominated by asphalt, gravel, and rusty tracks—this project proposes an urban transformation through programmatic redistribution. A central building becomes the anchor, while surrounding squares are assigned distinct functions to reintroduce public life and urban greenery.

RESEARCH QUESTION

How can program distribution and spatial typologies reactivate an abandoned rail yard into a public urban sequence?

KEY TAKEAWAY

Public life can be designed through sequences: anchor + typologies + program logic.

Urban regeneration Program distribution Public life
03

2018

Design Project

Abracadabra

Landscape + Interior Design / Under-viaduct Activation

Abracadabra activates an under-viaduct area in Corvetto, Milan, where sidewalks are neglected and people move through as mere passersby. Informed by Marc Augé's concept of "non-place" and references such as Kobo Abe's "Box," the project treats public discomfort and safety as design questions.

RESEARCH QUESTION

How can infrastructural leftovers shift passersby from transit-only roles into social participants with identity and agency?

KEY TAKEAWAY

Design can transform "non-places" by choreographing relationships, not only forms.

Non-place Identity Activation
